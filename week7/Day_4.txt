ğŸ§ª Exercise 3 â€” Background Workers (Core Concept)

Answer briefly:

What is a background worker? A background worker is a separate, independent process or thread 
that performs long-running or intensive tasks (like sending emails, generating reports, or syncing data) 
in the background, preventing them from freezing or slowing down the main application's user interface (UI).

Why are background workers used? Background workers are crucial for improving application performance, 
scalability, and reliability by offloading time-consuming tasks (like sending emails, processing data, or generating reports) 
from the main user-facing process, ensuring the front end remains fast and responsive for users.

What kinds of failures are background workers especially good at handling? Background workers are particularly effective 
at handling failures that involve retries of transient errors, such as temporary network outages or 
database connection time-outs. They are also well-suited for managing partial failures in long-running jobs, 
enabling the operation to resume from a saved state rather than restarting from scratch.
